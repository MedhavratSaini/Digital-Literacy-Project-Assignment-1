🛡️ Following is a Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students (India)
✅ Prevention Tips
Use strong, unique passwords for each account and enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.
Do not click on suspicious links in emails, SMS, or WhatsApp messages—even if they appear to be from trusted sources.
Verify website URLs carefully before entering login or personal details (look for “https” and correct spelling).
Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities like banking or logging into important accounts.
Keep your devices updated with the latest security patches and antivirus software.
Limit personal information shared on social media, such as your phone number, address, or college ID.
Be cautious of “too good to be true” offers, such as fake internships, giveaways, or job opportunities.
💳 Financial / UPI Safety Tips
Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or banking passwords with anyone—even if they claim to be from your bank.
Do not approve unknown “collect requests” on UPI apps; scammers often trick users into authorizing payments.
Double-check recipient details before sending money via UPI or banking apps.
🚨 Reporting Cybercrime
Report incidents immediately on the National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in
Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 for urgent financial fraud cases

Following these simple steps can significantly reduce your risk and help you stay secure in today’s digital world.
